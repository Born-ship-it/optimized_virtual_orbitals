\chapter{Results}
\label{ch:results}

In this chapter, the results of the OVOS implementation are presented. The chapter is structured as follows:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Section 6.1} validates the implementation by comparing full-space MP2 correlation energies against PySCF and literature values.
    \item \textbf{Section 6.2} presents the main result: the fraction of MP2 correlation energy recovered as a function of the active virtual orbital count.
    \item \textbf{Section 6.3} analyses the convergence behaviour of the OVOS algorithm.
    \item \textbf{Section 6.4} compares different initialization strategies and their impact on final energies and convergence.
    \item \textbf{Section 6.5} investigates how the choice of basis set affects energy recovery.
    \item \textbf{Section 6.6} provides a direct comparison with the results published by Adamowicz and Bartlett in their original OVOS paper.
    \item \textbf{Section 6.7} assesses the computational cost and scaling of the OVOS algorithm.
    \item \textbf{Section 6.8} examines the T1 amplitudes in the converged OVOS orbitals to test for Brueckner-like properties.
    \item \textbf{Section 6.9} estimates the practical benefits of using OVOS orbitals as a reference state for quantum computing applications, including overlap with FCI states and resource estimates for VQE.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{CO}

The OVOS algorithm results for CO in the cc--pVDZ basis through each $N'_{\rm act.unocc}$ to full space of active unoccupied orbitals are shown in Figure~\ref{fig:CO_cc-pVDZ_ovos}. OVOS recovers increasingly more correlation energy $E_{\rm corr}^{\rm OVOS}$ as $N'_{\rm act.unocc}$ increases, with the percentage of correlation energy recovered reaching 100\% at $N'_{\rm act.unocc} = 21$. Reaching 90\% recovery at $N'_{\rm act.unocc} = 13$, which is approximately 60\% of the total number of active unoccupied orbitals. 

We mark the difference the OVOS algorithm makes at each $N'_{\rm act.unocc}$ from initial point with iterations as the line connecting the points for each $N'_{\rm act.unocc}$, highlighted in Figure~\ref{fig:CO_cc-pVDZ_ovos} as the points for each $N'_{\rm act.unocc}$. The most recovered correlation energy is between $N'_{\rm act.unocc} = 3$ and $N'_{\rm act.unocc} = 10$ with $\Delta E_{\rm corr}^{\rm OVOS} \simeq 0.1$ Hartree. Convergence is nearly achieved in all cases, $N'_{\rm act.unocc} = 6$ being nonconvergened reaching maximum iterations. Iteration counts somewhat increases as $N'_{\rm act.unocc}$ reaches 50\% of the total number of active unoccupied orbitals and decreases thereafter. 

Shortcomings of the OVOS algorithm are observed at $N'_{\rm act.unocc} = 11, 12$ and $N'_{\rm act.unocc} = 13$ for start guess RHF, where the algorithm fails to converge at an agreeable correlation energy, and instead converges to a local minimum with a much higher correlation energy. This suggest that the orbital optimization landscape has multiple local minima, which can trap the algorithm if the initial guess is not close enough to the global minimum. This is further explored in Section 6.4, where the effect of different initialisation strategies is investigated.

The correlation energy iterations are plottet in Appendix~\ref{app:CO_cc-pVDZ_ovos_iterations} to show the convergence behaviour of the OVOS algorithm for CO in the cc--pVDZ basis at each $N'_{\rm act.unocc}$.

Brueckner orbitals are defined as the orbitals for which the single excitation amplitudes (T1) vanish, i.e. the excited determinants with one electron promoted from an occupied to a virtual orbital do not contribute to the wavefunction, see Section~\ref{sec:brueckner}.
